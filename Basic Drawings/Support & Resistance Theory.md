**Support:**
- Areas of demand. A price level where traders want to buy more shares. 
- Visually prices don't go LOWER than this level.

![](attachments/Pasted%20image%2020250328095934.png)

- Example: Every single time we come to this green line. 
- More buyer than sellers. 
- This is the level of support, and prices wont go lower.

![](attachments/Pasted%20image%2020250328100231.png)

**Supply and Demand and Value:**
- When supply is low and demand is high, that is a value assets and ask higher prices for the assets
- Same things hold true but in reverse for resistance.

**Support:**
Areas of demand. A price level where traders want to buy more shares. 
Visually prices dont go LOWER than this level
**Resistance:**
Ares of high supply, low demand. Want to sell. 
Visually prices dont go HIGHER than this level (the red line)

![](attachments/Pasted%20image%2020250328100347.png)

- Red line is our resistance 
- Every time price comes in, prices back off 
- More sellers than buyers 
- Supply is greater than demand 
- Resistance don't want to go higher than prices might be (red line)

# Key Principles

When support is broken; it becomes resistance.

When resistance is broken; it becomes support

# Three Key Steps To Drawing
- Pick the appropriate time frame your trade to draw levels. Always start on daily. 
- Draw levels with the most touches (data), go back and fine tune to maximize. 
- Use the zone effect to allow for wiggle room.
