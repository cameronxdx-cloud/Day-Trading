- Looking for price continuation in the direction of trend in place 
- A sign that the current trend should continue.
- If price is trending up; this may be a brief pause before continuing up. If trending down; this may be a brief pause before continuing down. 
- Used to trade the continuation OR avoid new trades.

## Bullish/Bearish Channel 

Easiest to spot and only requires a support and resistance trend line. 

Trend lines do not have to have perfect touches every time price reverses. 

Clear placement of orders for entry and exit 

![](attachments/Pasted%20image%2020250407143524.png)

Bullish: Want to be a **buyer** at **support**; **seller** at **resistance** 
Bearish: **Seller** first at resistance 

**What candles are forming in Bullish Channel?** 
- Reversal at every time you come into support trend line or resistance trendline 
- ![](attachments/Pasted%20image%2020250407143915.png)

**What candles are forming in a Bearish Channel?** 
- Inverted hammer candle

## Bullish / Bearish Flag Patterns 

Can be a flag or a pennant, the overall principle is the same for each formation.

Forms after a large move in one direction, buyers and sellers need a rest before the next move. Sometimes called consolidation patterns. 

Clear placement of orders for entry and exit.

![](attachments/Pasted%20image%2020250408132326.png)

- When you see large scale to the upside, lots of momentum at play

- Want momentum up higher

- Watch for bull flag to form about halfway (50%) to hold

**Volume:**
- When we see breakout in upward, we want that on high volume 

- When consolidating we want that to drop off; low volume 

**When you see an aggressive high upside, will little pull back, look for a potential flag pattern** 
Volume bars are strong during that spike up 

## Recap

**CONTINUATION PATTERNS** - Favor a continuation of the trend. Also known as consolidation patterns and reflect market participants pausing before next move.
