**Long (Bullish)**
- Buying first in hopes to sell at a higher price in the future, Want to see price go UP.

**Short (Bearish)**
- Selling first in hopes to buy at a lower price in the future. Want to see prices go DOWN.

**Volume**
- The amount of shares or contracts exchanged over a period of time.

**Price Action**
- The price of a security tracked over a period of time. 

**Indicators** 
- Upper
- Lower

**Resistance**
- It's like a "ceiling"—when the price approaches this level, many traders start selling, causing the upward momentum to slow down or reverse.

**Support**
- Areas of demand. A price level where traders want to buy more shares. 
- Visually prices don't go LOWER than this level.
- Think of it as a "floor"—when prices approach this level, traders and investors start buying, creating demand that stabilizes or reverses the downward trend.

**Retest**
- Occurs when the price of an asset revisits a previously identified level of support or resistance after breaking through it. Think of it as the market "double-checking" that the new level is valid.
